About LOK TARANG

Folk dances represent India's rich and diverse cultural heritage, passed down through generations.

This event encourages students to explore various folk dance forms from different states of India, building cultural awareness, confidence, and a deeper appreciation for our country's artistic traditions. Join us for an evening filled with rhythm, color, and celebration!

Competition Rules

01

Category

Folk Dance (Indian Folk Dance forms)

02

Music & Accompaniment

Folk dance music relevant to chosen dance form must be used

Folk Dance will be on Folk Song appropriate to folk tradition

Folk Dance song must be submitted in advance / brought on a pen drive (as instructed by organizers)

Any technical issues related to song will be responsibility of participants

03

Dance Form

Performances must represent authentic Indian folk dance styles

Inappropriate gestures or content will lead to disqualification

04

Attire

Participants are encouraged to wear traditional or folk costumes that reflect the origin and culture of chosen dance form

Costumes should be decent, safe, and suitable for a school competition

05

Time Limit

Stage setup and music setup: 2 minutes

Performance duration: 5 to 6 minutes

Participants must strictly adhere to time limit

Negative marking will be applied for exceeding the allotted time

06

Disqualification Criteria

Exceeding permitted number of participants

Use of inappropriate or offensive content

Misrepresentation of folk dance form

Failure to follow time limits or competition guidelines

Judging Criteria

Performances will be evaluated based on the following criteria

Rhythm & Coordination

Synchronization with music, timing accuracy, and coordinated movements among participants

Expression & Energy

Facial expressions, body language, enthusiasm, and emotional connection to the performance

Costume & Cultural Authenticity

Authenticity of traditional costumes, cultural representation, and visual appeal

Choreography & Formation

Creativity of dance movements, formations, transitions, and overall choreographic structure
